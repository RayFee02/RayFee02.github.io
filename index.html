Transmission Lag

Investigated the transmission of SBP monetary policy to commercial bank net interest margins (NIM) across Pakistan's top-5 listed banks over 10 years (2015–2024). Using 40 quarterly observations, applied OLS regression and first-difference analysis to isolate the policy rate effect from inflation, credit growth, exchange rate volatility, and NPL changes. Result: R² = 0.87, β = 0.42 — each 100bps rate increase associates with a 42bps NIM expansion with a one-quarter lag. A practical framework for equity analysts covering Pakistan's banking sector during rate cycles.

Key Findings
  • Sector NIM expanded from 3.2% (2015) to 7.1% (2023 peak), closely tracking the SBP rate cycle
  • OLS result: NIM = 1.84 + 0.42 × PolicyRate — R² = 0.87, p < 0.001, F-stat: 241.3
  • One-quarter transmission lag confirmed via Granger causality test
  • Rate cut cycle (22% → 12% in 2024) predicted ~420bps NIM compression — visible in Q3 2024 results
  • SCBP showed highest rate sensitivity (β = 0.54); Meezan lowest (β = 0.31) due to Islamic finance structure
  • Implication: banks with higher government security allocations outperform in rising rate environments

Constructed and optimized a 10-stock KSE-100 portfolio (LUCK, ENGRO, HBL, MCB, OGDC, PSO, NESTLE, UBL, MARI, MTL) using the Markowitz mean-variance framework in Python. Used 3 years of monthly PSX data (2021–2024) to compute a full 10×10 covariance matrix, then generated 10,000 Monte Carlo portfolios to map the efficient frontier. The max-Sharpe portfolio delivers 24.6% return at 15.8% volatility — Sharpe of 1.34 versus 0.89 for the equal-weight benchmark and 0.71 for the KSE-100 index.

Methodology & Results
  • Monthly adjusted prices for 10 stocks, 2021–2024 (PSX & Bloomberg); covariance matrix annualised ×12
  • 10,000 Monte Carlo portfolios generated; each scored by Sharpe ratio (Rf = 12% SBP rate)
  • Optimal sector weights: Banking 28%, Energy 22%, Fertilizers 18%, Cement 15%, Consumer 12%, Other 5%
  • Min variance portfolio: 16.8% return, 14.2% vol, Sharpe 0.98
  • SciPy optimization traced the analytical efficient frontier to verify Monte Carlo results
  • 50% Sharpe improvement over equal-weight; 89% over KSE-100 index

Structured an investment banking–style deal analysis of PTCL's landmark $400M acquisition of Telenor Pakistan and Orion Towers (completed December 31, 2025) — Pakistan's largest telecom consolidation in over a decade. Built a comparable transactions framework benchmarking the deal across 6 emerging-market telecom M&A transactions (2020–2025) on EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA, EV/Subscriber, and EV/MHz multiples. Assessed deal rationale, synergy drivers, regulatory risk (CCP Phase II conditions, PTA NOC), and accretion/dilution profile. Concluded the deal was fairly valued at the lower end of the EM comp range — offering strategic rather than financial upside, with EPS accretion expected by year 3 post-integration.

Methodology & Key Findings
  • Deal EV PKR 108B (~$400M) on a cash-free, debt-free basis — financed via 7-year IFC-led consortium debt facility
  • EV/Revenue: 1.8× vs. EM telecom comps median of 2.1× — deal priced at ~14% discount, consistent with Pakistan country-risk premium
  • EV/EBITDA: 5.3× (Telenor PK standalone) vs. comps range 4.8–7.2× — within fair value band
  • EV/Subscriber: ~$22 vs. EM median $19 — slight premium justified by Telenor's superior 4G data ARPU
  • Combined Ufone + Telenor = 35.4% subscriber market share; Jazz retains 38% — competitive duopoly dynamics modelled
  • Revenue synergy NPV: PKR 12–18B from network sharing, tower co-location (Orion Towers), and OPEX rationalisation over 5 years
  • Accretion/dilution: EPS-dilutive in year 1 due to debt servicing; accretive by year 3 assuming 12% synergy realisation rate
  • Regulatory risk: CCP Phase II conditions (wholesale access, spectrum sharing) reduce synergy upside by ~20% in bear case
  • Post-deal: PTCL (MergeCo) positions for upcoming 5G spectrum auction — strategic option value not captured in base case

  • SEARL: highest earnings sensitivity to deregulation + rate cuts; dominant in cardiovascular, pain, and gynaecology — Top Pick with OVERWEIGHT rating
